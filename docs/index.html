Large Language Models Fall Short: Understanding Complex Relationships in Detective Narratives
Runcong Zhao1*, Qinglin Zhu1*, Hainiu Xu1, Jiazheng Li1, Yuxiang Zhou1, Yulan He1,2, Lin Gui1
1King's College London, 2Alan Turing Institute
Paper Code Data

Existing datasets for narrative understanding often fail to represent the complexity and uncertainty of relationships in real-life social scenarios.

  • To address this gap, we introduce a new benchmark, Conan, to evaluate and understand the inference capacity of LLMs in complicated relationships.
  • It includes role-oriented relationships from the perspectives of various characters, incorporating
    • Public Relations known to most characters,
    • Secret Relations known to only a few,
    • Inferred Relations that are not explicitly stated in any character’s story but can be deduced by synthesising information from all characters collectively.

Our experiments with advanced Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-3.5, GPT-4, and Llama2 reveal their limitations in inferencing complex relationships and handling longer narratives. The combination of the Conan dataset and our pipeline strategy is geared towards understanding the ability of LLMs to comprehend nuanced relational dynamics in narrative contexts.
